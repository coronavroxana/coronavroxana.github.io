04 · Health Analytics

Chronic Disease Risk Prediction

CDC NHANES + BRFSS · Diabetes & Obesity · Dual-dataset comparison

CatBoost Mann-Whitney NHANES BRFSS Health Analytics
+ details

Problem & Why it matters

Can objective body measurements (waist, arm circumference, height) predict diabetes risk as accurately as a behavioral survey — without lab tests? This project compares two CDC datasets to answer that question, and introduces a novel anthropometric ratio (WAR) as a potential low-cost screening indicator.

AUC-ROC 0.81
CatBoost · NHANES anthropometrics · 2x precision vs behavioral model

Key Takeaways

Anthropometric measurements match behavioral survey predictive power (0.81 vs 0.80 AUC-ROC) with nearly double the precision (0.57 vs 0.29). WHR and WHeightR outperform BMI as risk indicators. Regression analysis confirms body measurements stratify population risk but cannot replace lab HbA1c for individual diagnosis — validating clinical practice.

→ View on GitHub

Methodology

  • 01 Loaded BRFSS (253,680 surveys) and NHANES 2021-2022 (6,715 participants with lab HbA1c); merged 4 clinical datasets via participant ID
  • 02 Engineered WAR (Waist-to-Arm Ratio), WHR, WHeightR; applied height-stratified median imputation for 912 missing values
  • 03 Mann-Whitney U hypothesis testing across 5 anthropometric ratios vs HbA1c — all significant (p < 1e-39)
  • 04 Trained CatBoost, Random Forest, Logistic Regression on both datasets with class imbalance handling
  • 05 Cross-dataset comparison on AUC-ROC and Precision; NHANES model achieves 2x precision with 38x fewer observations
  • 06 Regression analysis (Linear + RF) for HbA1c prediction; RMSE 0.94 confirms anthropometrics insufficient for individual diagnosis

Problem & Why it matters

Zyfra develops efficiency solutions for heavy industry. The goal: predict gold recovery yield across flotation and purification stages to eliminate non-profitable process parameters — directly analogous to pharma batch release validation and QA process control.

sMAPE 11.17%

Methodology

  • 01 Loaded and merged 3 datasets (train, test, full); verified gold recovery formula with MAE ≈ 0

  • 03 EDA across extraction stages — compared Au/Ag/Pb distribution, detected zero-anomalies
  • 04 Feature engineering: homologated train/test feature sets, StandardScaler applied post-split
  • 05 Trained Linear Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest; evaluated with custom sMAPE
  • 06 Final model selection via cross-validation; identified non-profitable parameters for operational review

02 · Capstone · End-to-end ML

Customer Churn Prediction

Interconnect Telecom · Full pipeline from raw data to business strategy

XGBoost CatBoost AUC-ROC Class imbalance Clinical transferable
+ details

Problem & Why it matters

Predict which telecom subscribers will churn so the marketing team can intervene proactively with retention offers. The same ML architecture transfers directly to patient dropout, clinical trial adherence, and subscriber retention in health platforms.

AUC-ROC 78.38%
XGBoost · holdout test set · 5-model comparison

Key Takeaways

Class imbalance doesn't always require resampling — class_weight='balanced' with proper cross-validation seeds preserved data integrity while improving minority-class recall. Feature source matters: contract type was the strongest churn predictor.

→ View on GitHub

Methodology

  • 01 Integrated 4 relational tables (contract, personal, internet, phone); unified into single analytical dataset
  • 02 EDA: distribution analysis, churn rate by contract type, tenure, and service bundle
  • 03 Feature engineering: encoded categorical variables, engineered tenure and service-interaction features
  • 04 Class imbalance handled via class_weight='balanced' and scale_pos_weight — no artificial resampling
  • 05 Trained 5 models: Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, CatBoost, XGBoost
  • 06 Final selection: XGBoost (AUC-ROC 78.38%) with cross-validation; translated risk scores into retention framework
